# Anti-Harassment and Non-Discrimination Policy

Acme Corp is committed to maintaining a workplace free from discrimination, harassment, and retaliation.

## Protected Characteristics

Acme Corp prohibits discrimination and harassment on the basis of:
race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age (40+), disability, genetic information, pregnancy, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, veteran status, marital status, or any other characteristic protected by applicable law.

## What Constitutes Harassment

**Harassment** includes unwelcome conduct that is based on a protected characteristic and that:
- Creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment, or
- Unreasonably interferes with work performance.

Examples include: offensive jokes, slurs, epithets, mockery, insults, threats, intimidation, ridicule, or unwanted physical contact.

**Sexual harassment** includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature.

## Reporting

Employees who experience or witness harassment should report it **immediately** through any of these channels:
1. Their manager (if the manager is not the subject of the complaint)
2. Any HR Business Partner
3. The Ethics Hotline (anonymous): 1-800-555-ETHICS
4. The legal department: legal@acme-internal.com

All complaints are investigated promptly, thoroughly, and as confidentially as possible.

## Non-Retaliation

Retaliation against anyone who reports harassment in good faith or participates in an investigation is strictly prohibited. Retaliation is itself a serious violation of this policy and will result in disciplinary action.

## Consequences

Substantiated harassment may result in disciplinary action ranging from mandatory training to immediate termination, depending on severity. Severe or physical harassment may be reported to law enforcement.

## Training

All employees must complete the **Preventing Harassment in the Workplace** training module within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter. Managers must complete an additional leadership module covering investigation obligations.
