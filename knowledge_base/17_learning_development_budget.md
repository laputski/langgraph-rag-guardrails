# Learning and Development Budget

Acme Corp invests in employee growth through a dedicated Learning and Development (L&D) budget.

## Annual L&D Stipend

Every full-time employee receives an **annual L&D budget of $2,000** per calendar year, available from January 1st. The budget resets each January 1st and does not carry over.

Part-time employees (20–39 hours/week) receive a prorated budget of **$1,000/year**.

New hires receive the full annual amount from their start date through December 31st (not prorated mid-year).

## Eligible Expenses

The L&D budget may be used for:
- **Online courses and certifications**: Coursera, Udemy, Pluralsight, AWS/GCP/Azure certifications, PMP, CFA, etc.
- **Books and publications**: technical books, industry journals, ebooks (up to $200/year in this category without additional approval)
- **Conferences**: registration fees for industry conferences (travel and accommodation require separate manager approval and use travel expense policy)
- **External coaching or mentoring**: with manager and HR approval
- **Tuition reimbursement**: undergraduate or graduate coursework relevant to your role — see the Tuition Assistance program for additional funding up to $5,250/year (IRS tax-free limit)

## Non-Eligible Expenses

- General hobby courses unrelated to your role
- Entertainment (concerts, sports events) even if framed as networking
- Certifications that are prerequisites for your current role (should be covered at hire)

## How to Use the Budget

1. Discuss the learning plan with your manager to ensure alignment with your development goals.
2. Submit a request in Workday under **L&D → Budget Request** before purchasing.
3. Most requests under $500 are auto-approved if within budget. Requests over $500 require manager sign-off.
4. After completing the course, submit a brief (1-paragraph) summary of learnings in Workday.
5. Reimbursements are processed via Concur — attach receipt and the Workday approval reference.

## Tuition Assistance

Employees pursuing a degree while employed may receive up to **$5,250/year** in tuition assistance (in addition to the $2,000 L&D budget). A minimum grade of B (or equivalent) is required. Employees who leave within 12 months of receiving tuition assistance must repay a prorated portion.
