# Code of Conduct

Acme Corp's Code of Conduct defines the standards of behavior expected of every employee, contractor, and business partner.

## Core Principles

1. **Integrity**: Act honestly and transparently in all business dealings. Never misrepresent facts, products, or our company to customers, partners, or regulators.
2. **Respect**: Treat every person with dignity, regardless of their role, background, or viewpoint.
3. **Accountability**: Take ownership of your actions and their outcomes. Speak up when you see something wrong.
4. **Confidentiality**: Protect the company's confidential information and that of our customers and partners.

## Conflicts of Interest

Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflict of interest to their manager and HR. Examples include:
- Outside employment or consulting that competes with or involves Acme Corp customers
- Financial interest in a company that does business with Acme Corp
- Supervising a direct family member or romantic partner

## Gifts and Entertainment

- Employees may not accept gifts valued at more than **$100** from any vendor, customer, or business partner.
- Meals and entertainment are acceptable when they serve a legitimate business purpose and are reasonable in value.
- Cash or cash equivalents (gift cards) of any amount are prohibited.

## Use of Company Resources

Company resources — including computers, email, phones, and office space — are provided for business purposes. Incidental personal use is permitted, but employees should have no expectation of privacy on company systems.

## Reporting Violations

If you witness or suspect a violation of this Code, you must report it:
- To your manager or HR Business Partner
- Via the anonymous Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-ETHICS or ethics.acmecorp.com
- Retaliation against anyone who reports in good faith is strictly prohibited and is itself a violation of this Code.

## Consequences

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, and may be referred to law enforcement where applicable.

## Acknowledgment

All employees must acknowledge receipt and understanding of the Code of Conduct annually via Workday.
